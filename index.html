Wildfire Intensity and Distribution Map

About the Chart

This map displays wildfire data across various regions, featuring 5-degree grid lines and clearly marked state borders. Each circle represents a wildfire, with the size of the circle indicating the fire’s magnitude, and its color reflecting the intensity. A slider allows you to filter the data, making it easy to focus on stronger or weaker wildfires as needed.

Key Observations:

  • More wildfires are detected near the coast.
  • NSW, QLD, and NT tend to be especially vulnerable to wildfires.
  • The majority of detected fires have low intensity, with FRP values below 500.
  • The highest detected fire in NSW had nearly double the FRP of the second highest.

Customizable Slider: Filter data by adjusting the threshold for fire intensity.

Interactive Tooltips: Hover over points to see details like date and fire size.

Color and Size Encoding: Colors and sizes represent fire intensity and size.

Legend: Understand data with the help of a color-coded legend.

Wildfire Risk Heatmap

About the Heatmap

This heatmap shows fire risk across states by month, using a diverging colour scheme of green to red to represent low to high risk.

Key Observations:

  • Winter months are mostly green (low risk), while summer turns red (high risk).
  • SA and NT show the highest fire risk during summer.

Interactive Tooltips: Hover to view fire risk values by state and month.

Parallel Analysis of Bushfire Risk Metrics

About the Parallel Plot

This parallel plot compares bushfire risk metrics across Australian states. Each line represents a state, highlighting variations in temperature, wind speed, humidity, and pressure.

Key Observations:

  • NSW has the highest max temperature and wind speed, indicating higher bushfire risk.
  • Tasmania shows lower pressure and higher humidity, suggesting lower risk.

Interactive Tooltips: Hover to see data points and corresponding metric values.

Hover Highlighting: When hovering over a state line, it is highlighted for easy identification and comparison.

Distribution of Bushfire Types

About Forest Areas

This chart displays the distribution of total and burnt forest areas across different types of forests in various states. It allows users to explore wildfire impacts on different forest types interactively.

Key Observations:

  • NSW has a higher burnt area than total forest.
  • NT has a lower burnt area than total forest.
